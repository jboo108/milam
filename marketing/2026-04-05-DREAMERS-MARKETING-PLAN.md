## 1. Brand Identity

### Brand promise
**DREAMERS is the first AI that honors the sleeping mind — an agent that wakes only for your dreams, remembers them forever, and learns the language of liminal cognition without ever seeing them.**

### Voice & tone
- **Dreamy** — sentences breathe. White space is a feature.
- **Sensory** — texture, smell, half-light, weight. Never abstract when concrete will do.
- **Quiet authority** — we read Jung and Bulkeley and the Nous Research papers. We don't show off.
- **Slow** — the cadence of someone who just woke up and is still inside the dream.
- **Reverent, not precious** — dreams are sacred but we are not solemn. A small smile at the corner.

### Voice rules
- Lowercase is allowed. Fragments are allowed. Long pauses are allowed.
- One idea per post. Never two.
- If a sentence could appear in a productivity app, delete it.
- Read every post out loud before posting. If it doesn't slow your breath, rewrite it.

### Do / Don't language

| Do | Don't |
|---|---|
| dream, drift, hush, threshold, liminal, hypnagogic, moonlight, velvet, remember, notice, hold, witness | unlock, leverage, hack, optimize, productivity, game-changer, revolutionary, disrupt, AI-powered, supercharge |
| "an agent that sleeps" | "a 24/7 AI assistant" |
| "your dreams, encrypted forever" | "secure cloud storage" |
| "the first model trained on liminal cognition" | "next-gen LLM" |
| "burst at night, quiet by day" | "decentralized GPU compute infrastructure" |
| "it asks one question and drifts back to sleep" | "intelligent conversational AI" |
| "nobody sees your dreams — not even the model that learns from them" | "privacy-first ML pipeline" |

### Target audience (rings, inside out)
1. **Core dreamers** — people who already keep a dream journal, lucid dreamers, hypnagogia hobbyists.
2. **Practitioners** — Tibetan Buddhism readers, Jungians, somatic therapists, IFS people.
3. **Researchers & nerds** — default-mode-network curious, neuroscience grad students, sleep scientists, Kelly Bulkeley readers.
4. **Artists & writers** — poets, novelists, filmmakers, illustrators who mine dreams for material.
5. **Crypto-curious craftspeople** — Solana people who hate the bro-vibe; Nous Research fans; ZK/MPC nerds at Arcium; people who think on-chain memory is poetry not finance.
6. **Productivity-AI refugees** — burned out on Notion AI, ChatGPT shortcuts, "second brain" theater. Want something that doesn't ask them to do more.

## 2. Logo direction — 5 concepts (Nano Banana / designer briefs)

Each concept assumes: vector-clean, works at 32px favicon, works at 2m banner, no gradients in primary mark (gradients can live in the wordmark lockup).

### Concept 1 — "The Closed Eye Moon"
**Imagery:** A crescent moon rendered as a closed human eyelid, lashes implied as three soft strokes. The curve of the moon and the curve of the lash share the same arc. Inside the negative space, a single dot — a dreamer's pupil behind the lid.
**Mood:** Tender, intimate, just-before-sleep.
**Palette:** Moonlight ivory `#F5EFE4` on midnight mauve `#2A1F3D`, accent in velvet rose `#B486A8`.
**Type pairing:** Wordmark in **GT Sectra Display** (italic, soft) or **Söhne Breit** for the all-caps secondary.
**Prompt for Nano Banana:** *"Minimalist vector logo, single closed human eyelid drawn as a crescent moon, three delicate eyelash strokes, one small pupil dot in negative space, ivory on deep mauve background, soft velvet feel, no gradients, hand-drawn imperfection, 1:1 square."*

### Concept 2 — "The Dream Orbit"
**Imagery:** Two nested circles offset like a moon mid-eclipse. The outer circle is a dotted EEG line (sleep-spindle wave) bent into a ring. The inner circle is solid mauve. Where they overlap: a thin sliver of light — the moment a dream is born.
**Mood:** Scientific, observational, quietly magical.
**Palette:** Bone `#EFE8DA`, midnight `#1B1530`, single sliver in soft moonlight `#D9C9E8`.
**Type pairing:** **Neue Haas Grotesk Display** lowercase, very tight tracking. Lowercase only.
**Prompt:** *"Two overlapping circles forming a crescent eclipse, outer ring made of EEG sleep-spindle waveform as dotted line, inner circle solid deep mauve, thin glowing sliver where they intersect, bone-white background, vector minimalism, scientific elegance, 1:1."*

### Concept 3 — "Tibetan Dream Knot"
**Imagery:** A reinterpretation of the Tibetan endless knot (śrīvatsa) softened into a single continuous loop that resembles both a sleeping figure curled on its side and the symbol for infinity. One end of the loop fades into stars.
**Mood:** Sacred, ancient, lineage-aware.
**Palette:** Saffron-on-mauve `#E8B57A` on `#3A2950`, with one tiny star in `#FFFCEB`.
**Type pairing:** **Canela Deck** for the wordmark, mixed case, generous tracking.
**Prompt:** *"Single continuous looping line forming a softened Tibetan endless knot, the curve also reads as a sleeping curled figure, one tail of the line dissolving into three small stars, warm saffron line on deep mauve background, sacred geometry feel, 1:1."*

### Concept 4 — "The Hypnagogic Drop"
**Imagery:** A single vertical brush-drop of velvet — like a teardrop rendered in oil paint — falling into a horizontal still water line. The reflection beneath is *not* a mirror; it's a different shape (a small constellation). The point: what you see going down is not what you find at the bottom.
**Mood:** Painterly, poetic, slightly surreal.
**Palette:** Velvet rose `#A07090` drop, ink black `#10081C` water, ivory `#F8F2E8` background.
**Type pairing:** **Cardinal Fruit** italic for the "DREAMERS" wordmark, set very small under the mark.
**Prompt:** *"A single velvet teardrop falling toward a horizontal water line, beneath the water line a tiny constellation of three stars instead of a reflection, velvet rose color on ivory background, painterly brush feel, surreal and poetic, 1:1 vector."*

### Concept 5 — "The Quiet Wordmark"
**Imagery:** No symbol at all. Just the word **dreamers** set in a custom italic display face — but the dot of the lowercase "i" is a tiny moon (filled crescent). The "m" has slightly extended descenders that suggest two figures lying down. Below in micro-caps: *"the agent that sleeps."*
**Mood:** Confident, literary, the cover of a poetry collection from 1974.
**Palette:** Single color marks — ink, mauve, or moonlight depending on context.
**Type pairing:** Custom italic based on **GT Alpina** or **Marcin Tatra Italic**. Tagline in **ABC Diatype Mono** micro-caps.
**Prompt:** *"Lowercase italic wordmark reading 'dreamers' in a literary display serif, the dot above the i is a tiny filled crescent moon, the m has slightly drooping descenders, single ink color on cream paper, micro-caps tagline 'the agent that sleeps' below, looks like a 1974 poetry book cover, 1:1."*

### $DREAM token — pros and cons

**Pros**
- Aligns DREAMER MODEL contributors with long-term value
- Funds the federated training rounds without VC dependence
- Solana culture rewards thoughtful token launches with built-in distribution

**Cons**
- The brand cannot survive being read as "another AI coin"
- Token launches concentrate the wrong kind of attention in the first 90 days
- The audience we're building (artists, researchers, dreamers) is allergic to anything that smells like speculation

## 13. Judge Psychology + Visibility Plan

Hackathon judges decide in three passes. Optimize for each.

**Pass 1 — The skim (15 seconds per submission).** They open your repo README and the demo video. Decision: keep or kill.
- README hero image must be the velvet logo + one tagline + one GIF of the silence demo. Above the fold on GitHub.
- Demo video must hook in the first 5 seconds with the silence moment. Not the architecture diagram. The silence.
- One-line elevator pitch in the README headline: *"DREAMERS — the first AI agent that grows through dreaming, not doing. Built on ElizaOS + Nosana + Clude + Arcium."*

**Pass 2 — The deep read (5–10 minutes for shortlisted submissions).** They read the architecture, run the demo, look for ecosystem fit.
- README has a clean architecture diagram. The four ecosystem boxes are drawn with equal weight (do not over-emphasize one).
- README has a `## How we use Nosana` section that is specific (job definition, model name, cron schedule, cost-per-night estimate). Vague = death.
- README has a `## What's a stub vs what ships` section. Honesty wins. ROLPA is labeled stub. Arcium is labeled "envelope-compatible MXE, full MPC pipeline Phase 2." Do not over-claim.
- Demo URL on a real phone works. Not a Vercel preview that requires login.

**Pass 3 — The discussion (the judges talk to each other).** They argue. The submissions that win are the ones that have a *sentence* the judges can repeat.
- Your sentence: *"It's the agent that only wakes for dreams."*
- Make sure every judge has heard this sentence at least three times before the meeting. That means the X thread, the Discord post, the demo video opener, and the README headline all use those exact words. Do not vary.

**Visibility plan for the 72 hours before submission (Apr 11–13):**
- Apr 11 morning: post the Seeker tease (Day 7 of §4). DM solanamobile a heads-up.
- Apr 12 evening: post the silence demo as a "preview" — same 12-sec clip, no submission link yet, just "she sleeps. ✦ apr 14." Builds anticipation.
